# Compass Research Planning

Use Compass when OCEAN needs to turn evidence gaps into research plans, experiment designs, idea cards, journal positioning, collaboration routes, or proposal revisions.

Compass is not free-form brainstorming. Its ideas must be anchored to inspected evidence, missing evidence, reviewer pressure, trend movement, resource gaps, or validation needs.

## Purpose

- Convert Sounding, Current, Reef, Iceberg, and Anchor outputs into feasible next moves.
- Prioritize ideas by evidence need, risk, novelty, feasibility, and publication or collaboration value.
- Design experiments, analyses, proposal aims, or manuscript revision strategies.
- Clarify what the user can contribute.

## Trigger

Read this file when the user asks about:

- research ideas, proposal design, next experiments, aim design;
- what to do after a paper review or reviewer comment;
- journal positioning or manuscript strategy;
- collaboration strategy or contribution planning;
- turning evidence gaps into publishable work.

## Inputs

Compass can start from:

- idea seed or one-sentence hypothesis;
- Sounding source packet;
- Current trend map;
- Reef resource/provenance map;
- Iceberg claim gaps;
- Anchor validation plan;
- reviewer comments or public review pressure signals.

## Safety Rules

- Do not present an idea as novel without a bounded Current search.
- Do not make feasibility claims without resource and validation constraints.
- Do not turn reviewer comments into facts.
- Do not promise publication tier, acceptance, authorship, or collaboration outcome.
- Keep idea strength proportional to evidence state.

## Workflow

1. **Planning question**
   - Identify whether the user needs idea generation, proposal revision, experiment design, journal strategy, or collaboration route.
2. **Evidence anchors**
   - List the source packet, trend gap, resource issue, claim gap, or validation gap driving the plan.
3. **Idea generation**
   - Create bounded options:
     - conservative;
     - balanced;
     - ambitious;
     - rescue/revision option if needed.
4. **Feasibility and risk screen**
   - Score each option for evidence fit, novelty risk, data need, validation burden, execution effort, and publication or collaboration value.
5. **Select route**
   - Recommend one route, one backup route, and what evidence would change the decision.
6. **Handoff**
   - To Harbor for decision memo and collaboration boundary.
   - Back to Sounding/Current/Reef/Anchor if evidence is too thin.

## Stop Conditions

Stop or route back when:

- there is no traceable evidence anchor;
- novelty depends on an unperformed search;
- feasibility depends on unavailable data, code, or collaborators;
- the user asks for guaranteed publication, authorship, or grant success;
- the plan would require clinical or biological claims not yet supported.

## Failure Modes

- Brainstorming disconnected from evidence gaps.
- Overvaluing novelty without Current search.
- Designing experiments that do not test the core claim.
- Treating proposal polish as scientific validation.
- Ignoring user contribution boundary.
